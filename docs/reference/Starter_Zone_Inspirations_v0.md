# Starter Zone Inspirations v0

**Agent:** Reference Curator  
**Date:** 2025-03-11  
**Status:** Draft  
**Purpose:** Extract starter zone design lessons from legacy MMOs for Avalon 1–10 zone architecture  
**Sources:** Classic WoW (2004), TBC (2007), Wrath (2008), Old School RuneScape (2007), ArcheAge Classic, Ashes of Creation (concept)

---

## Questing

| Lesson | Source | Avalon Application | Tag |
|--------|--------|-------------------|-----|
| **Kill 10 rats teaches combat basics** — Low-stakes repetition builds muscle memory without punishment | WoW Elwynn/Kharanos | Zone 1: 8–10 "practice" mobs before any quest-giver speaks; no failure state, no repair costs | Must |
| **The "breadcrumb" quest** — NPC walks you to next hub, establishing geography naturally | WoW Barrens crossroads | Zone 1 hub → Zone 2 hub escorted by wounded scout; player sees path, not map | Must |
| **Choice without consequence** — OSRS lets you pick dialogue paths that flavor, not lock, your journey | OSRS Tutorial Island | Optional flavor replies in Zone 1-2 dialogue; builds agency early | Later |
| **Gather 5 copper ore > kill boss** — Resource gathering teaches economy before combat escalation | ArcheAge starter zone | Zone 1: 60% gathering/crafting quests, 40% combat; invert by Zone 5 | Must |
| **Fail-forward design** — OSRS tutorial deaths are free, teaching without shame | OSRS Tutorial Island | Zone 1: No death penalty, no corpse runs, no "you died" guilt screens | Must |

## Economy

| Lesson | Source | Avalon Application | Tag |
|--------|--------|-------------------|-----|
| **Copper matters** — Starting with 0 gold makes 5 copper feel earned | WoW/TBC starting bags | Zone 1: Players start with 0 coin; first 10 copper = first "wealth" dopamine hit | Must |
| **Everything has a price** — Even gray items sell; vendor trash teaches value | WoW/Runescape | Zone 1 vendors buy literally anything; no "useless" drops, only "less valuable" | Must |
| **The first "big" purchase** — Saving for 1g bag upgrade creates first economic goal | WoW 10-slot bags | Zone 2: 8-slot bag for 2g; players grind 20 mins consciously for first expansion | Must |
| **Local mats, local value** — ArcheAge trade packs required zone-to-zone transport | ArcheAge early trade | Zone 1-2: Resources only valuable in adjacent zones; no global AH access yet | Later |
| **Gold sinks before gold faucets** — Repair costs exist before dailies flood economy | WoW Classic | Zone 3 introduces repair + food costs; Zone 5+ introduces dailies/"faucets" | Must |

## Social

| Lesson | Source | Avalon Application | Tag |
|--------|--------|-------------------|-----|
| **Forced proximity** — Single quest objective = 5 players waiting = conversation starts | WoW boar meat hell | Zone 1: 3–4 bottleneck quests requiring same mob/item; no instancing, no phasing | Must |
| **The "helping hand" buff** — High levels passing by buff lowbies; social goodwill | WoW/tBC buffs | Zone 1-2: Players can cast 1hr buffs on anyone; visible icon = social proof | Later |
| **No level scaling = visible hierarchy** — Seeing a Level 60 creates aspiration | Classic WoW | Zone 1: No scaling; Level 50+ players = walking billboards for "what's possible" | Must |
| **Guild invitation at Level 5** — Early belonging reduces churn | WoW guild spam era | Zone 1: NPC suggests "finding companions"; optional guild board with active listings | Later |
| **Resource competition drives interaction** — ArcheAge land rushes created drama | ArcheAge launch | Zone 3: First "contested" mining nodes; not PvP, but "I got here first" tension | Later |

## Atmosphere

| Lesson | Source | Avalon Application | Tag |
|--------|--------|-------------------|-----|
| **Day/night audio shift** — Elwynn day birds vs. night crickets = place memory | WoW zone ambience | Avalon Zone 1: 3 distinct ambient layers (day/dusk/night) with 30-min crossfades | Must |
| **The "safe" music** — Starter zones use major key, predictable progressions | WoW Elwynn/Durotar | Zone 1 soundtrack: Recognizable 4-bar motif; when it returns later, triggers nostalgia | Must |
| **Footstep fidelity** — Different terrain = different sounds; builds spatial awareness | Ashes of Concept (APOC tests) | Zone 1-2: 6+ footstep surfaces; player learns to "hear" terrain without looking | Later |
| **Weather as event** — Rain isn't constant; it's memorable when it arrives | OSRS/Runescape 3 | Zone 2-3: 10% chance of rain/storm; NPCs comment on it; immersion proof | Later |
| **Distant landmark tease** — Seeing Stormwind from Elwynn = future promise | WoW line-of-sight design | Zone 1: High points reveal Zone 5-6 terrain; "you'll go there eventually" | Must |

## Foe Reveal (Slow-Burn Enemy Buildup)

| Lesson | Source | Avalon Application | Tag |
|--------|--------|-------------------|-----|
| **"Just wolves" for 5 levels** — No urgent threat; player masters basics first | WoW Elwynn defias arc | Zone 1-2: Only natural beasts, no humanoids; Defias-equivalent appears Zone 3 | Must |
| **The "odd" corpse** — Dead wolf with strange wounds before seeing the cause | Wrath Borean Tundra (undead reveal) | Zone 2: Dead mobs with "strange markings"; Zone 4 reveals cause | Must |
| **NPC fear before player danger** — Quest text warns of "something in the mines" | WoW Echo Ridge/Forests | Zone 3 NPCs: Whisper about caves; player enters at Zone 4 with dread built | Must |
| **Nameless before named** — "Gnoll" for 3 levels, then "Hogger" as culmination | WoW gnoll arc | Zone 2-3: Generic "Bandit"; Zone 4 introduces named lieutenant with backstory | Must |
| **Environmental storytelling** — Burned houses before seeing the army | TBC Hellfire/HFP | Zone 3-4: Scorched earth, abandoned camps; Zone 5 reveals who/why | Later |
| **The "too strong" glimpse** — High-level enemy one-shots you as warning | WoW wandering elites | Zone 4: Level 30+ "Elite" enemy patrols road; teaches respect, not engagement | Later |

## Summary: Non-Negotiables (Must)

1. **Zero start, slow climb** — 0 coin, 0 gear, 0 shortcuts
2. **Nature before civilization** — Beasts before humanoids, practice before stakes
3. **Audio identity** — Day/night ambience, distinct footstep surfaces, memorable music motif
4. **Social friction** — Shared objectives, no phasing, visible hierarchy
5. ** breadcrumb geography** — Never open full map; show the path, not the destination
6. **Earned fear** — Environmental warnings before named threats
