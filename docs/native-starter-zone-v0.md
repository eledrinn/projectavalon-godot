## Executive Summary

This document defines the Native 1–5 starter zone: **Ashroot Cradle**, a protected ancestral village and its immediate surroundings in the stable wetlands of the greater basin. The zone teaches players the Native worldview (wetland-rooted, highland-adapted, anti-hubris) while establishing the seamless overworld philosophy and flowing logically toward the convergence city path.

---

## Phase 1: Geography & Flow

### Zone Boundaries

**Ashroot Cradle** occupies a stable shelf within Region 3 (Native Wet Basin). It is bounded:

| Direction | Boundary | Beyond |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| **East** | Mirrored shallows and root channels framed by drowned redwood roots | Deepens into the full wet basin (Levels 6–10) before grading into the Redwood Transition Belt |
| **West** | Foothill rise, ridged stone | The climb toward convergence city (Levels 8–10) via the Foothill Rise terraces |
| **North** | Ancient reed forests, fog banks | Uncapped expansion territory |
| **South** | Fungal shelf groves, gas pocket fields | Transition to redwood belt approaches |

**Zone Size:** Approximately 1.2km × 1.5km playable area (roughly 2-3x classic MMO starter zone scale to support seamless philosophy).

### 1–5 to 6–10 Transition Points

The zone has **three natural exit vectors** that flow into the broader 6–10 basin experience:

1. **The Drowning Road (East)** — A flooded causeway of ancient stone that gradually deepens into marsh channels. Players cross waist-deep water, signaling the end of the protected cradle. The final bends elevate just enough to give a sideways glimpse of the Redwood Transition Band beyond the basin, foreshadowing the shared eastward routes.

2. **The Stair-Terraces (West)** — Carved stone ramps ascending toward the foothills. Visual preview of convergence city architecture. First glimpse of mountain-scale verticality. These terraces are the in-world onramp to the Foothill Rise region, letting players feel the slope change without a loading break.

3. **The Gas-Touched Path (South)** — Shallow route through fluorescent fungal shelves. More dangerous, rewards exploration with early glimpse of redwood transition ecology.

### Sub-Areas (5 Distinct Zones)

#### 1. Stillwater Village (Levels 1–2)
*The protected ancestral hub*

- **Elevation:** Slightly raised earth-and-root shelf above seasonal flood levels
- **Character:** Intimate, safe, tutorial space
- **Visuals:** Low earth-hugging roundhomes with woven reed roofs, root-embedded foundations, heavy use of softened wood and volcanic stone
- **Key Feature:** Central **Hearthroot** — a massive partially-exposed root system forming the village communal gathering space

#### 2. The Miregarden (Levels 2–3)
*Transition from safety to wild*

- **Elevation:** Sunken, waterlogged channels and hummocks
- **Character:** First "wild" space, still gentle
- **Visuals:** Shallow mirrored pools, floating fungal pads, channel-crossings via woven rope bridges or stepping-stone root ridges
- **Key Feature:** **Shellmound Ridge** — an ancient elevated burial/ritual site of packed shell and stone, now overgrown with flowering wetland plants

#### 3. The Ashroot Thicket (Levels 3–4)
*Dense, spiritually significant grove*

- **Elevation:** Mixed, with dramatic root-terraces rising 10–15m
- **Character:** Mystical, ancient, first hint of danger
- **Visuals:** Massive vertical root systems (the "Ashroots" — fossilized/stone-like remains of pre-Ash megafauna feeding roots), fungal luminescence, hanging fiber offerings
- **Key Feature:** **The Woven Arch** — two massive root pillars joined by centuries of accumulated fiber-wrappings, forming a natural gateway

#### 4. The Gaspocket Flats (Levels 4–5)
*Hazard boundary zone*

- **Elevation:** Low, flat, with sudden sink-holes and vents
- **Character:** Dangerous beauty, requires attention
- **Visuals:** Fluorescent gas pockets drifting between fungal shelves, iridescent film on standing water, warning-marker stones carved with old symbols
- **Key Feature:** **The Breathing Holes** — periodic gas vents that players must time movement around (early environmental hazard teaching)

#### 5. The Terrace Foot (Level 5)
*Transition to the greater world*

- **Elevation:** Stepped stone rising toward the western foothills
- **Character:** Cultural crossroads, first view of "new ways" influence
- **Visuals:** Mixed architecture (old wetland motifs on newer stone construction), wider paths, first view of distant mountain terraces
- **Key Feature:** **The Waymarker** — a carved stone pillar indicating distances to Stillwater Village, the Convergence City (Verdant Spire), and the Ash Frontier

### Verticality Strategy

| Area | Vertical Range | Design Purpose |
|------|----------------|----------------|
| Stillwater Village | 0–3m | Grounded, intimate, safe |
| Miregarden | -1–2m | Slight sinking, water immersion |
| Ashroot Thicket | 0–15m | Root-climbing, first vertical exploration |
| Gaspocket Flats | -2–1m | Sinking danger, gas layers at different heights |
| Terrace Foot | 0–20m | Ascending toward the greater world |

**Seamless Connection Philosophy:** No hard boundaries. Each area blends into the next via intermediate transition strips (30–50m of mixed ecology).

#### Seamless Regional Connections

- **Eastward to the Redwood Transition Band:** The eastern shallow shelves carry increasing driftwood, rust-toned pollen, and drowned redwood stumps. By the time players step off the Drowning Road, the mirrored pools are reflecting the first towering trunks, creating a visual handshake with Region 2 while keeping footing consistent with wet-basin traversal mechanics.
- **Westward to the Foothill Rise:** The Terrace Foot stones literally key into the first Foothill Rise ridges. Water channels narrow, root cover thins, and stone ribs emerge, letting players climb 15–20 meters before the 6–10 Foothill encounter spaces kick in. This preserves seamless streaming while telegraphing the culture shift toward highland life.

## Phase 2: Landmarks & Identity

### The Starter Hub: Stillwater Village

**Naming Status:** `TENTATIVE` — "Stillwater" captures the wetland memory; alternatives: **Ashroot Village**, **Reedhaven**, **Hearthroot**. Final naming decision pending creative director review.

> **GLOSSARY CALL:** `Stillwater Village` — Protected ancestral Native village in the wet basin; 1–5 starter hub. *Proposed for GLOSSARY.md, status: PENDING.*

**Population:** ~40–60 inhabitants visible (background population, not all interactive).

**Architecture Principles (per Peoples_Natives canon):**
- Low, earth-hugging roundhomes with organic rooflines
- Root-embedded foundations (buildings grow *from* the land, not placed *on* it)
- Soft asymmetry — no perfect geometries
- Materials: woven reed thatch, softened driftwood frames, volcanic stone bases, fiber-tie construction
- Softened leathers hung as doorway coverings, wind-flaps, rain-guards

### Primary Landmarks

#### 1. Hearthroot — The Living Heart

> **GLOSSARY CALL:** `Hearthroot` — Central communal root-shrine in Stillwater Village; gathering space formed by massive exposed root system. *Proposed for GLOSSARY.md, status: PENDING.*

**Visibility Notes:**
- **Sightline Dominance:** Visible from any point in Stillwater Village; root crown rises 8–10m above village floor
- **Smoke Signal:** Perpetual thin smoke threads rising through root channels; visible from Miregarden approach
- **Audio Signature:** Low thrum of communal drums, shell chimes in root-fingers
- **Night Presence:** Faint fungal luminescence (blue-amber) along root channels creates warm glow visible after dark

**Cultural Meaning:**
The Hearthroot represents the **relationship principle** at the heart of Native identity — that community is not constructed but *grown*, that shelter comes from working *with* the land rather than imposing upon it. The exposed root channels (through which smoke and light filter) symbolize transparency and collective breath — nothing is hidden, all participate.

**Storytelling Hooks:**
- Players return here for milestone rites (level-ups, class confirmations)
- Root channels echo with "memory whispers" — ambient audio of ancestral voices (subtle worldbuilding)
- Seasonal festivals see the entire village gathered; players witness the scale of community they're entering

---

#### 2. The Woven Arch — Threshold of Commitment

> **GLOSSARY CALL:** `The Woven Arch` — Sacred gateway formed of wrapped root pillars; marks boundary between protected cradle and wild lands. *Proposed for GLOSSARY.md, status: PENDING.*

**Visibility Notes:**
- **Approach Indicator:** Visible from 200m within Ashroot Thicket; root pillars rise 12–15m, creating natural frame
- **Distance Read:** From Terrace Foot, visible as vertical marker against the sky; helps orient navigation
- **Dynamic Appearance:** Fiber offerings shift with seasons and recent player actions (new offerings appear after significant quests)
- **Audio Layer:** Wind through fiber wrappings creates distinctive low harmonic (musical threshold)

**Cultural Meaning:**
The Arch embodies **intentional passage**. Unlike arbitrary zone boundaries, this is a *chosen* threshold — the community has wrapped these roots for generations, each fiber representing a prayer, a memory, a binding promise. To pass through is to accept relationship with what lies beyond. The Arch teaches that danger is not the enemy; *unmindful* danger is.

**Storytelling Hooks:**
- First passage triggers "threshold vision" — brief glimpse of the ancestral spirit who first wrapped the roots
- Returning through the Arch from the wild triggers different NPC dialogue ("You've walked beyond; what did you learn?")
- Offerings can be added by players (small persistent world change)

---

#### 3. Shellmound Ridge — The Ancestor's Vista

> **GLOSSARY CALL:** `Shellmound` — Ancient elevated ritual/burial site of packed shell and stone; ancestral memory made physical. *Proposed for GLOSSARY.md, status: PENDING.*

**Visibility Notes:**
- **Vantage Dominance:** Highest point in 1–5 zone (15m elevation); visible from all sub-areas except deepest Miregarden channels
- **Silhouette Recognition:** Distinct conical mound shape against skyline; flower-covered slopes visible as color patch (white/purple seasonal blooms)
- **Landmark Navigation:** Used by NPCs as reference point ("Meet me when the sun clears the Shellmound")
- **Approach Scale:** Appears larger as players climb; false summit effect rewards ascent with expanding vista

**Cultural Meaning:**
The Shellmound contains **compressed time** — thousands of years of meals, rituals, burials packed into elevated earth. It represents continuity and the weight of relationship across generations. For Natives, elevation equals memory; what is raised up is remembered. The flowers that now grow there (brought by wind and bird) represent the ecosystem's participation in remembrance.

**Storytelling Hooks:**
- Quiet contemplation at summit triggers "ancestral resonance" — vision of pre-Ash landscape (educational worldbuilding)
- Certain quests require dawn or dusk presence at Shellmound (solar alignment)
- Site of the single Severed encounter in Level 4—subtle corruption of the sacred

**Reference Digest Integration (Peoples_Natives + Starter_Continent_Slice):**

| Material | Source Location | Visual Treatment | Cultural Significance |
|----------|----------------|------------------|----------------------|
| **Woven Reed Thatch** | Local wetland harvest | Soft, weathered gold-brown; organic curves; asymmetrical overhangs | Humility — grows from the place, returns to it |
| **Softened Driftwood** | Basin channels, flood deposits | Gray-smooth, no bark, water-worn edges | Patience — the water shapes what time cannot rush |

| **Shell & Bone** | Shellmound harvest, respectful gathering | White, pearl, amber patina | Remembrance — the ancestors' gift |
| **Fungal Luminescence** | Gas pocket colonies | Soft blue-green, amber, non-electric glow | Mystery — the land's own light |
| **Mineral Bloom** | Skin pigmentation of inhabitants | Subtle iridescent undertones in NPC complexions | Adaptation — carrying the place in the body |

**Visual Identity Summary:**

**The Native Starter Zone Must Feel:**
- **Rooted** — everything grows from or sinks into the land
- **Intimate** — human-scale spaces, close vegetation, enclosed sightlines
- **Ancient** — old stone, older roots, accumulated ritual layers
- **Beautiful but Uneasy** — gorgeous fungal luminescence and mirrored pools, but gas pockets and Ashroot scars hint at old wounds
- **Materially Rich** — reeds, fibers, softened wood, volcanic stone, shell, bone, feather

**Color Palette:**
- **Greens:** Deep reed, moss, algae film (not bright grass)
- **Browns:** Softened wood, earth, leather, root
- **Grays:** Volcanic stone, ash-dusted surfaces
- **Accents:** Shell white/pearl, fungal luminescence (soft blue-green, amber), mineral bloom in skin

### NPC Archetypes & Landmark-Tied Storytelling

> **Quest Designer Input:** The following archetypes are anchored to specific landmarks, creating spatial storytelling that reinforces cultural learning through place-based repetition.

#### 1. Elder Rootwalker (Hearthroot Anchor)

**Location:** Rotates between Hearthroot gathering space and nearby roundhomes.

**Archetype:** The Old Ways conscience — cautious, patient, speaks in root-metaphors and seasonal cycles.

**Landmark Ties:**
- **Hearthroot:** Teaches communal fire-tending rite; explains smoke-channel significance
- **Story Hook:** "The roots remember what the trunk forgets" — quest chain about recovering pre-Ash songs from root-channel echoes
- **Conflict:** Opposes hasty adoption of "new ways" tools; players navigate between reverence and practicality

**Quest Beats:**
- Level 1: Fire-tending tutorial (timing, patience, community service)
- Level 3: Intercedes at Shellmound when "new ways" returnee suggests mechanized dredging
- Level 5: Blesses player before Terrace Foot departure; final dialogue reflects player choices (old-ways leaning players receive root-blessing)

#### 2. The Shellmound Watcher (Shellmound Anchor)

**Location:** Permanent presence at Shellmound Ridge; tends flowers, maintains path markers.

**Archetype:** Ancestral intermediary — quiet, observant, sees patterns across time.

**Landmark Ties:**
- **Shellmound:** Lives in small shelter at mound base; knows burial layers, can identify shell types by touch
- **Story Hook:** "The flowers grow where they will; we only clear what chokes" — quest about invasive fungal rot threatening ancestral blooms
- **Revelation:** Watcher is youngest of line stretching back to original mound-builders; carries genetic memory (subtle Bonder-like trait)

**Quest Beats:**
- Level 2: Tend ancestral offerings; teaches ritual mechanics through humble labor
- Level 4: Site of single Severed encounter—Watcher's ancestral sensitivity detected the disturbance
- Level 5: Provides warning that must be carried to Verdant Spire

#### 3. The Ashroot Speaker (Woven Arch Anchor)

**Location:** Small platform-nest built into The Woven Arch itself; rarely leaves.

**Archetype:** Bonder lore-keeper — speaks of the "old binders" and their relationships with relic beasts.

**Landmark Ties:**
- **Woven Arch:** Mediates between village and wild; maintains fiber offerings
- **Story Hook:** "The Arch was not built; it was convinced" — explains how generations of careful binding persuaded the roots to grow together
- **Bonder Lore:** Deep foreshadowing of binding traditions without granting actual abilities in 1–5

**Quest Beats:**
- Level 3: Guides first passage through Arch; ritualized threshold moment with "vision of the first binder"
- Level 3: Shares "The Speaker's Lesson" — pure lore about beast lineages, consent philosophy
- Level 5: Extends formal invitation to binding training (actual companion acquisition begins in 6–10)

#### 4. The Terrace Scout (Waymarker Anchor)

**Location:** Terrace Foot, near the Waymarker.

**Archetype:** New ways ambassador — represents convergence city interests, political complexity.

**Landmark Ties:**
- **Waymarker:** Diplomatic parley point; players choose how to frame warning for Verdant Spire
- **Story Hook:** First point of contact with the wider world's complexity
- **Transition Function:** Funnels all players toward Verdant Spire for 6–10

**Quest Beats:**
- Level 5: "The Waymarker Parley" — choose message framing (military/diplomatic/ritual warning)
- Level 5: "Terrace Duties" — humble preparation tasks before departure
- Level 5: Facilitates the departure transition (Verdant Spire selectable, Ash Frontier teased but locked)

## Phase 3: Quest Arc Summary

**Full Documentation:** `/projectavalon/Docs/GameDesign/Quest/Native_Starter_Quest_Arc_v0.md`

### Overview

The 1–5 quest arc teaches players **what it means to be Native** through humble participation rather than heroics. Each level band focuses on everyday tasks—chores, channel tending, escort duties—that build understanding through action.

**Key Design Shifts:**
- **Humble focus:** Early quests are grounded (tending channels, gathering reeds, delivering messages) rather than heroic
- **Deferred class power:** Bonder lore is foreshadowed throughout, but actual binding questline begins post-level 5 in Verdant Spire
- **Single exit path:** Waymarker funnels all players to Verdant Spire; Ash Frontier is teased but locked
- **Minimal Severed presence:** Only one direct encounter in 1–5 (Level 4); future pacing keeps threat light through level 10, escalating only in late-game

### Level-by-Level Breakdown

| Level | Zone Focus | Key Landmark | Quest Focus |
|-------|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| 1 | Stillwater Village | Hearthroot | Humble chores: tending channels, gathering reeds, delivering messages |
| 2 | Miregarden | Shellmound Ridge | Escort duties, tending offerings, clearing channels |
| 3 | Ashroot Thicket | The Woven Arch | Maintenance tasks, lore lessons, vertical exploration |
| 4 | Gaspocket Flats | Shellmound Ridge | Hazard navigation, **single Severed encounter**, routine clearing |
| 5 | Terrace Foot | The Waymarker | Preparation, class unlocks, departure to Verdant Spire |

### Class Hook Summary

**Bonder (Mandatory Lore, Deferred Power):**
- Levels 1–4: Philosophy and lore foreshadowing through Elder Rootwalker and Ashroot Speaker
- Level 3: "Vision of the first binder" and deep lore about beast lineages
- Level 5: Formal invitation to binding training; actual companion acquisition begins in 6–10

**Ranger (Optional Depth):**
- Levels 2–4: Optional tracking opportunities and environmental clues
- Level 5: Basic tracking vision unlocked

**Forceshaper (Optional Depth):**
- Levels 2–4: Optional temporary structure previews (reed pylons, root anchors)
- Level 5: First temporary structure blueprint unlocked

### Old vs New Ways Tension

Quests throughout present choice points where players navigate between traditional and practical approaches:

| Level | Choice Context | Old Ways | New Ways |
|-------|---------------|----------|----------|
| 1 | Hearthroot Rite dialogue | Reverence, patience | Curiosity, efficiency |
| 2 | Shellmound tending | Full ritual | Practical shortcut |
| 4 | Stranger encounter | Investigate | Drive off |
| 5 | Waymarker message | Ritual warning | Military alert |

**Important:** Neither is framed as purely right or wrong. Choices feel subtle—NPCs remember and respond organically, not through loud morality meters.

### Transition to 6–10

**The Waymarker Departure:**
- **Verdant Spire** (primary) — Selectable by all players; political, social complexity
- **Ash Frontier** (teased only) — Visible but locked; "Return when you have walked longer paths"
- **Return to Cradle** (temporary) — Optional completionist return

**Severed Pacing Note:**
- **1–5:** Single mysterious encounter + rumors
- **6–10 zone:** Light sightings, distant figures, corrupted wildlife
- **Level 10 zone:** First real skirmishes, scout parties
- **Late game:** Heavy Severed presence, infiltration missions

This boss represents the first major Severed confrontation—a corrupted manifestation or servant, not a direct Severed infiltrator. The true nature of Severed infiltration (methods, hierarchy, goals) remains mysterious until late-game content, preserving the "emerging threat" pacing.

### Assumptions for Technical Architect Review

1. **Zone Streaming:** The 1.2km × 1.5km zone should stream seamlessly with adjacent 6–10 areas.

2. **Verticality Support:** Root-climbing in Ashroot Thicket requires mantling/vaulting mechanics or designated climb points.

3. **Gas Pocket System:** Gaspocket Flats requires either:
   - Periodic hazard zones with visual/audio warning
   - Particle-based danger areas
   - Or simpler: visual-only (luminescence) with no damage

4. **Root Architecture:** Hearthroot and Woven Arch require custom art assets (large root structures with fiber wrapping).

5. **Seamless Boundaries:** The three exit vectors (Drowning Road, Stair-Terraces, Gas-Touched Path) need technical validation for seamless transition to 6–10 terrain.
