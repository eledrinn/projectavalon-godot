# Travel and Mounts v1

**Status:** LOCKED — Mounts late, glider early via questline

## Overview
Travel pacing is a core design pillar. The world should feel large early. Mounts are earned, not given. Early vertical slice provides alternative traversal (gliders) that doesn't trivialize the world.

---

## Mounts (LOCKED)

### Unlock Timing
- **Status:** Post-level 10 or very late in vertical slice
- **Philosophy:** Mounts are meaningful rewards, not conveniences
- **Effect:** Player experiences world on foot first, appreciates speed later
- **World scale:** Early zones designed for foot travel (distances, quest density)

### Mount Design Principles (TENTATIVE)
- Cultural variants (Native, Arrival, Severed mount types)
- Not just speed — possibly utility (climbing, water, etc.)
- Upgrade/customization path
- Earned through questing/reputation, not vendor purchase

---

## Gliders (LOCKED)

### Concept
**"The First Flight"** — a substantial questline rewards the player's first glider.

### Function
- Traverse from high elevation to lower areas
- Descent tool, not ascent tool
- Enables shortcuts, exploration, and vertical world design
- Does not trivialize terrain (can't fly up)

### Questline Design (TENTATIVE)
- **Length:** Long-ish (multiple sessions, not one afternoon)
- **Location:** Likely convergence city or post-1–10 content
- **Theme:** Mastery of local terrain, earning trust/favor, proving worthiness
- **Cultural flavor:**
  - **Native:** Taming/fabricating from relic beast materials, learning wind currents
  - **Arrival:** Engineering a personal descent device, sacred geometry of air
  - **Severed:** Salvaging pressure-based descent tech, jury-rigged survival gear

### Mechanics (TENTATIVE)
- Deploy from elevated position
- Controlled descent (steerable, not just fall)
- Stamina/energy limit (can't glide forever)
- Landing skill matters (rough landings = damage)
- Upgradable (better glide ratio, faster deployment, etc.)

---

## Early Game Travel (1–10)

### Primary Travel: Foot
- Walk/run through seamless overworld
- Experience terrain, landmarks, distance
- Shrines as checkpoints (death respawn, not fast travel)

### Limited Fast Travel
- **Status:** TENTATIVE — possibly shrine-to-shrine unlock later
- **1–10:** Minimal or none (world should feel traversed)

### Travel as Experience
- The journey is content, not padding
- Scenic routes, hidden paths, discovery rewards
- Group travel encouraged (4-player parties)

---

## World Design Implications

### Zone Design
- Distances designed for foot travel in 1–10 slice
- Verticality used with gliders in mind (post-10)
- Points of interest visible from distance (encourages exploration)

### Content Pacing
- Quest hubs spaced for foot travel
- Return trips (corpse runs, turn-ins) respect distance
- Glider shortcuts as rewards for reaching high places
