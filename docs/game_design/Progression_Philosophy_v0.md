# Progression Philosophy v0

## Status
LOCKED — Hybrid progression model: Cards (major build choices) + Talent Trees (classic branching)

## Progression Model Overview (LOCKED)

### Two-Layer System
**Layer 1: Progression Flavors (Cards)**
- Constellations, Tongues, Aspects, Chants, Etchings, Strings
- Major build-defining choices
- Found in world through exploration, quests, rituals
- Limited "deck slots" — choose which to equip at rest points
- Sidegrade emphasis: options, not pure power increases

**Layer 2: Talent Trees (Classic Branching)**
- Classic WoW-style branching talent trees
- Minor tuning, passive bonuses, ability modifiers
- Points awarded per level
- Respec available (with cost or at specific locations)
- Examples:
  - "Increases fire damage by X%"
  - "Reduces cast time of Y ability"
  - "Adds Z effect to W skill"

### Stat Model (LOCKED)
- **Main stats:** Stamina, Intelligence, Strength, Agility (names TBD)
- **Secondary stats:** Critical, Haste, Mastery, Versatility equivalents (names TBD)
- **Set bonuses:** Gear sets provide gameplay-changing effects
- **Gear vs Class:** Equal weight — neither eclipses the other

## Core Rule
Progression should emphasize:
- identity
- sidegrades
- discovery
- class expression
- world integration

It should not rely primarily on:
- endless raw stat inflation
- gear treadmill alone
- generic number growth without meaning

## Desired Feel
Players should feel:
- weak at first
- curious
- increasingly competent
- attached to their class
- tied to place and world
- proud of meaningful unlocks

## Progression Pillars
1. Class identity deepens over time
2. The world teaches and rewards attention
3. Unlocks matter as much as numbers
4. Exploration and class fantasy feed progression
5. The player’s sense of place should grow with power

## Class Progression Flavors (Cards System)
These are major build-defining choices — collectible, equippable, discovered in world.

### How Cards Work
- **Discovery:** Found through exploration, class quests, rituals, rare encounters
- **Equipping:** Limited slots (deck size), changed at rest points/shrines
- **Build impact:** Major gameplay changes (ability modifiers, new effects)
- **Collection:** Many cards exist, choose which fit your build

### Per-Class Flavor

| Class | Flavor | Card Examples |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| **Apogist** | Constellations | "The Weaver" — finisher pattern becomes square instead of triangle; "Collapse" — gravity spells pull enemies inward |
| **Tephrate** | Tongues | "Tongue of the Sulphur Vent" — vents become cone AoE; "Tongue of the Ash Fall" — pressure builds faster but vents cost health |
| **Bonder** | Aspects | "Aspect of the Crag Stalker" — bonus damage from elevation; "Aspect of the Mist Wader" — water walking, bonus healing in wetlands |
| **Forceshaper** | Chants | "Chant of the Four Winds" — confluence sequence can include wind twice; "Chant of the Deep Current" — water spells heal allies |
| **Armsman** | Etchings | "Etching of the Iron Wall" — blocking generates Openings; "Etching of the Dancing Blade" — dodge rolls cost less stamina |
| **Ranger** | Strings | "String of the Northern Gale" — arrows arc less, travel faster; "String of the Patient Hunter" — standing still increases crit chance |

### Talent Trees (Classic Branching)
Each class has 3 talent trees (names TBD per class):
- **Tree A:** Offensive/Damage focus
- **Tree B:** Defensive/Tank focus  
- **Tree C:** Utility/Support focus

**Spending:** 1 point per level, ~51 points by level cap
**Respec:** Available at trainers or special locations for cost
**Passive focus:** +% damage, -% cooldown, proc chances, etc.

### Combined Example: Bonder Build
- **Cards equipped:** Aspect of the Crag Stalker + Aspect of the Mist Wader
- **Talent points:** 31 in Tree A (damage), 20 in Tree C (utility)
- **Playstyle:** Mobile striker who uses elevation and wetlands, switches aspects for terrain

These should not be buried under generic gear score logic.

## Sidegrades vs Vertical Power
The game should lean toward:
- sidegrades
- specialized unlocks
- situational power
- identity-rich rewards

Rather than:
- only bigger stats
- endless percentage creep
- universal best-in-slot logic too early

## Gear Philosophy (LOCKED)
Gear and class progression have **equal weight**. Neither eclipses the other.

### Stat Design
- **Main stats:** Stamina, Intelligence, Strength, Agility (names TBD)
- **Secondary stats:** Critical, Haste, Mastery, Versatility equivalents
- **Tertiary stats:** Movement speed, life on hit, etc. (minor)

### Set Bonuses (Gameplay Changing)
Gear sets provide effects that modify how you play:
- (2-piece) "Your Opening attacks generate 1 additional combo point"
- (4-piece) "Constellation finishers refund 20% mana when completed perfectly"
- (6-piece) "While above 80% health, your Tongues cost no Pressure"

### Item Quality Tiers (Subject to Naming)
- **Common:** Stats only
- **Uncommon:** Stats + one secondary
- **Rare:** Stats + secondaries + small bonus effect
- **Epic:** Stats + secondaries + set bonus eligible
- **Legendary:** Unique effects that enable new builds (rare, chase items)

### Philosophy
- Gear provides the numbers and set bonuses
- Cards/Talents provide the playstyle customization
- Both required for optimal performance
- Sidegrades exist at all tiers (different stat distributions, different set bonuses)

## 1–10 Slice Philosophy
The first vertical slice should feel like:
- meaningful growth
- learning the world
- learning the class
- unlocking a few identity-defining tools
- entering the convergence city with a real sense of journey completed

Players should not outgrow the world instantly.

## What Should Drive Progression Early
- class unlocks
- landmark discovery
- class quests
- world quests rooted in place
- role expression
- local gear improvements
- social contact and travel
