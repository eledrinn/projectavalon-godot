# Economy & Trade Philosophy v0

## Core Principles

**Trade as Cultural Contact**
- Economy reflects the frontier tension between Arrivals, Natives, and Severed
- Markets are meeting grounds — places of curiosity, suspicion, and reluctant cooperation
- Value is cultural as well as material: what's precious to one group may be common to another

**Regional Specialization**
- Each sub-area has distinct gathering opportunities based on biome and history
- No single hub provides everything; travel and trade routes matter
- The Ash Frontier yields rare/dangerous materials; the Wet Basin yields abundant organics

**Three Economic Tensions**
1. **Arrival Order** — formalized trade, standardized currency, contract-based
2. **Native Reciprocity** — relationship-based, gift economy traces, reputation matters
3. **Severed Scarcity** — survival-focused, barter for necessities, secrecy premiums

## Currency & Exchange

- **Primary**: Mixed acceptance of Arrival minted coin (standardized) and native trade goods
- **Secondary**: Barter remains common in frontier regions
- **Forbidden/Severed**: Some goods only trade in shadow markets

## Trade Flows

- **Downhill**: Refined goods, tools, preserved foods, Arrival luxuries
- **Uphill**: Raw materials, organic compounds, volcanic minerals, native crafts
- **Cross-region**: Information, courier contracts, rare reagents

## Economic Safety Valves

- Regional price fluctuation based on supply/demand
- Vendor specialization prevents homogeneous "general stores"
- Courier loops enable arbitrage and player-driven trade opportunities
