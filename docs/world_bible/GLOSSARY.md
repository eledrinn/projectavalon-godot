# Glossary

**Status:** LOCKED terms with definitions. New terms require human approval.

## Peoples & Cultures

### The Arrivals
A long-lived or semi-immortal people from another world. They come from a sacred-classical civilization shaped by law, proportion, metaphysical order, and non-interference. The modern Arrivals return to this world through the Landing to confront their lost kin, the Severed.

### The Natives
A wetland-rooted people who later became heavily highland-adapted after the Age of Ash. Their civilization is a fusion of old wetland memory and later mountain survival. They are not anti-magic; they are anti-hubris.

### The Severed
The descendants/survivors of an offshoot branch of Arrivals who reached this world through the Fall. They abandoned reliance on old power and survived through secrecy, engineering, containment, and adaptation. They use fantasy-industrial technology (pressure, steam, cable, geothermal, alchemical fuel).

## Historical Events

### The Fall
The violent, catastrophic magical transit through which the first Arrival offshoot reached this world. In Native memory, it is remembered as a mythic event — some saw them as saviors or gods, others as oathbreakers.

**Contrast with:** The Landing (controlled, modern, deliberate)

### The Landing
The more controlled transit through which the modern Arrivals return to this world, long after the Fall. It is deliberate and expeditionary, not catastrophic.

**Contrast with:** The Fall (catastrophic, ancient, mythic)

### The Age of Ash
A world-shaping catastrophe that occurred shortly after the Fall, likely tied to massive volcanic or supervolcanic activity. Its scars remain visible in sulfuric valleys, ash basins, calderas, and altered ecosystems.

## Geography

### Western Continent
The continent where the game begins. It consists of two landmasses (northern and southern) divided by a narrow channel with volcanic islands.

### Northern Landmass
The larger of the two western continent landmasses. The starter slice is on its east coast.

### City Spur Range
An eastern offshoot of the true main mountain chain, where the first convergence city is built. This preserves the deeper main range for later exploration.

### Ash Frontier
The region beyond the convergence city, characterized by volcanic danger, fumaroles, caldera edges, and the first signs of Severed extraction/fortification.

## Game Systems

### Constellations
The Apogist progression flavor — discovering rarer celestial patterns tied to the world's skies.

### Tongues
The Tephrate progression flavor — uncovering forbidden languages of power.

### Aspects
The Bonder progression flavor — bonding with ancient surviving wildlife lineages.

### Chants
The Forceshaper progression flavor — formal techniques encoding elemental manipulation.

### Etchings
The Armsman progression flavor — weapon and combat philosophies expressed through markings.

### Strings
The Ranger progression flavor — shot philosophy and draw/release mastery.

## Production Terms

### Hard Pillars
The 10 non-negotiable project principles defined in Production_Rules.md. These cannot be changed without explicit human approval.

### Vertical Slice
The initial 1–10 level implementation focused on the starter frontier region. All features must justify themselves for this slice or the long-term foundation.

### Seamless Overworld
The technical principle that there are no loading screens between outdoor regions. Regions are design regions, not hard-loaded map boundaries.

### Globe-Compatible
The architectural principle that the world is treated as a planetary body from day one, even if the first implementation is locally flat. Future circumnavigation must remain technically possible.

## Conceptual Terms

### Pressure (Disambiguation)
The term "pressure" appears in multiple contexts with distinct meanings:

- **Tephrate Pressure:** A magical/metaphysical buildup of volcanic force, vented for tanking or damage. Combat mechanic.
- **Severed Technology:** Physical pressure systems (steam, hydraulics, pneumatic) powering fantasy-industrial machinery. No electricity.
- **Apogist Gravity-Pressure:** Celestial/gravitic pressure as part of gravity-collapse themed abilities. Cosmic force expression.

These are separate systems. Severed technology is not Tephrate magic. Apogist cosmic pressure is not mechanical.
