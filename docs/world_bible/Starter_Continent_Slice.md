# Starter Continent Slice v0

## Status
Locked broad geography. Many names still open.

## Macro World Placement
This project begins on the western of the world’s two main continents.

The western continent is itself a paired landmass:
- a larger northern landmass
- a smaller southern landmass
- a narrow channel between them
- volcanic islands scattered in the channel

The starting slice is on the east coast of the northern landmass at a somewhat northerly latitude.

## Core Geography Logic
The continent should feel geologically coherent and large.

The most important structural rule:
the main mountain chain sits relatively close to the eastern coast, creating:
- wet eastern slopes and basins
- visible mountains from the coast
- strong river logic
- a rain-shadow interior for later expansion

The first major city does not sit on the true main chain.
Instead, it sits on an eastern offshoot spur of that main range.
This preserves the true deeper mountains for later exploration.

## East-to-West Biome Chain
The core starter slice flows like this:

ocean → alien redwood coast → wet fungal basin / ancestral homeland → foothills → city spur range → convergence city → ash frontier → distant true main range

This sequence should feel seamless and logical.

## Region 1: Arrival Coast
### Function
- Arrival player start
- introduces Arrival identity and expedition tone
- establishes coastal scale and inland promise

### Environment
- curved bay or inlet
- partial cliffs
- black-sand or unusual shore material where appropriate
- alien redwood forest directly inland
- sea mist and strong sightlines toward mountains

### Starter Subzone
A protected Landing enclave equivalent to a Northshire or Valley of Trials scale intro area.

## Region 2: Redwood Transition Belt
### Function
Transition between coast and basin.

### Environment
- towering alien redwood analogues
- red or rust-toned canopy
- drifting spores or pollen
- damp root systems
- stream channels
- occasional sulfur-tainted runoff

This is not necessarily a full separate gameplay zone, but it is a major visual/ecological band.

## Region 3: Native Wet Basin
### Function
- Native player homeland and early route
- central basin identity
- ecological and cultural cradle

### Environment
- marsh channels
- giant roots
- standing water
- fungal shelves and groves
- fluorescent gas pockets
- shallow mirrored pools
- old stone ridges or shelf-islands
- relic wildlife grounds

### Starter Subzone
A protected ancestral village in a stable wetland cradle area.

## Water Logic
Rivers descend from:
- the city spur
- and more distant highlands/main chain

Some rivers drain directly east to the sea.
Others slow into the wet basin before reaching the coast as estuary channels.

This helps justify the basin as a rich, marsh-heavy homeland.

## Region 4: Foothill Rise
### Function
Transition from cradle to wider world.

### Environment
- ridged foothills
- stream cuts
- rocky shelves
- narrowing roads
- longer sightlines
- basin views behind and mountain shelf ahead

This is where player routes begin to converge more strongly.

## Region 5: City Spur Range
### Function
Accessible dramatic highland branch of the larger range.

### Environment
- eastern branch range off the true main chain
- terraces
- cliffs
- pass roads
- hanging bridges
- elevated shelves

This gives the first city dramatic mountain placement without exhausting the main range.

## Region 6: Convergence City
### Function
- first major shared hub around level 8–10
- native political and cultural center
- point of visible cultural overlap and tension
- city of old ways and new ways

### Placement
On a broad elevated shelf or pass in the city spur range.

### Views
- east: basin and distant sea
- west: higher ridges and hints of true mountains
- south: possible glimpses toward the inner channel and volcanic islands
- north: further terraces and future upland routes

## Region 7: Ash Frontier
### Function
- preview of level 10+ world
- first clear Severed pressure
- volcanic danger and old scars

### Environment
- fumaroles
- black-red rock
- caldera edges
- volcanic uplands
- glass fields
- industrial scars
- first clear signs of Severed extraction or fortification

This should not yet be the deepest heart of the continent, only the first warning.

## Region 8: True Main Range
### Function
Reserved for later exploration.

### Notes
This is the true mountain backbone of the northern landmass.
It should feel larger, colder, more continuous, and more imposing than the city spur.

Players should understand that the first city is only on a branch of something far greater.

## Southward Logic
The southern edge of the starter slice should imply the inner channel and the smaller southern landmass.

This can be done with:
- sloping coasts
- broken southern shorelines
- distant island silhouettes
- rumors or vistas of volcanic islands and sea passages

## Expansion Logic
### North
Cooler forests, higher ridges, further coast, later alpine or redwood variants.

### West / Inland
True main range, rain-shadow lands, harsher volcanic interiors, deeper Severed territory.

### South
Channel routes, island chains, southern landmass, maritime or volcanic expansions.

### Offshore / Global
Future continents, larger oceans, world-scale travel.

## Design Rule
This is one seamless overworld slice, not a set of disconnected maps.
Regions are design regions, not hard loading boundaries.
