# Peoples: Natives v0

## Status
Locked direction, with room for naming and detail refinement later.

## Core Identity
The Native player people are not simply “mountain people.” They are originally a wetland-basin people whose ancestral homeland was scarred and partially abandoned after the Age of Ash, driving many communities into the highlands.

Their modern civilization is therefore a fusion of:
- old wetland-rooted memory
- later highland survival and adaptation

They are not anti-magic. They are anti-hubris, anti-domination of sacred force, and deeply shaped by place, memory, ritual, and warning.

## Emotional Read
They should feel:
- rooted
- intimate
- ancient
- practical
- spiritually serious
- adaptive
- alive to danger
- culturally layered

They should not feel:
- primitive
- noble savage
- generic tribal fantasy
- cartoon beast-people
- blue alien cliché
- copy-pasted mountain civilization

## Physical Identity
They are humanoid and readable in a stylized MMO art style, but distinct from the Arrivals.

### Key physical traits
- grounded stance and body language
- slightly denser or more centered silhouette than the Arrivals
- strong facial structure
- practical hair and ornament logic
- subtle ash-mineral bloom patterning in the skin

### Notes
The key is not dramatic nonhuman anatomy. The key is:
- silhouette
- posture
- facial language
- material language
- subtle environmental adaptation

## Skin / Surface Logic
The strongest unique trait is a faint mineral or ash bloom patterning in the skin.

This should feel like:
- humid mineral marbling
- ash-toned veining
- subtle natural patterning, not magical tattoos
- more visible in heat, humidity, or ritual preparation if useful

It can appear strongest around:
- face
- shoulders
- collarbone
- hands
- upper back

This helps them feel native to a volcanic, wet, old world without making them cartoonishly alien.

## Hair / Ornament Language
Hair and adornment should carry wetland-rooted memory.

Strong directions:
- heavy braids
- wrapped cords
- reed or fiber ties
- carved stone/mineral clasps
- relic-beast bone or feather elements
- plume or frill motifs inspired by ancient birds and wetland life

## Material Language
This is a major part of the Native identity.

Use:
- reeds
- woven fibers
- softened leathers
- carved volcanic stone
- shell/mineral accents
- fungus-dyed cloth
- relic-beast bone or feather details
- polished wet wood
- root and bark forms

The Native silhouette should feel materially integrated with land and old life.

## Clothing Language
Clothing should reflect:
- damp climate history
- ritual significance
- practical layering
- later mountain adaptation

Key notes:
- wraps and layered cloth
- asymmetry is acceptable and often desirable
- practical belts, ties, and carrying methods
- ceremonial pieces should feel made, worn, inherited, and repaired

## Architecture Language
Native architecture should not be one-note.

### Wetland ancestral architecture
- low, earth-hugging
- rounded
- root-embedded
- partially grown into place
- organic rooflines
- soft asymmetry
- village logic that feels protected and nestled

Good shorthand:
organic, root-and-earth fantasy village, but not literal Hobbit imitation

### Highland architecture
- terraces
- stepped stone
- rope bridges
- shrine markers
- carved passes
- high platforms
- weathered civic construction

The mountain city should still visibly carry wetland memory in motifs and materials.

## Cultural Split
One of the strongest Native themes is the split between:

### Old ways
- basin rites
- wetland shrines
- relic creature kinship
- slower traditions
- ancestral land memory

### New ways
- mountain adaptation
- terrace civilization
- urban political life
- increased exchange with foreigners
- survival through structured change

This split should be visible in settlements, factions, and daily life.

## Relationship to Other Peoples
### Toward Arrivals
Mixed and varied:
- curiosity
- suspicion
- reverence in some lines
- resentment in others
- practical cooperation in many cases

### Toward Severed
Filtered through myth:
- some see them as old gods
- some as false gods
- some as betrayers
- some as tragic figures

## Visual Summary
The Native people are wetland-rooted and later highland-adapted humanoids whose visual identity is built from:
- grounded silhouette
- strong facial structure
- subtle ash-mineral skin bloom
- root/reed/fiber/bone/mineral material language
- old wetland memory carried into mountain civilization

## Avoid
- blue skin as primary identity
- giant fantasy ears/horns/tails
- obvious animal-people tropes
- generic tribal fantasy coding
- overdesigned “alien” features that break shared MMO gear readability
