# Peoples: Arrivals v0

## Status
Locked direction, with room for naming and refinement later.

## Core Identity
The Arrivals are a long-lived or semi-immortal people from another world.

They come from a sacred-classical civilization shaped by:
- law
- ritual
- proportion
- celestial order
- measured form
- ancient doctrine

They are not sci-fi.
They are not just “space elves.”
They are an old high fantasy civilization whose order is expressed through sacred geometry, astronomy, ceremonial craft, and disciplined social form.

## Emotional Read
They should feel:
- elegant
- composed
- formal
- precise
- disciplined
- old
- lawful
- expeditionary

They should not feel:
- futuristic
- technologically advanced in a sci-fi way
- generic Roman or Greek cosplay
- generic high elf clone
- coldly sterile

## Physical Identity
Compared to the Natives, the Arrivals should feel:
- taller or more elongated in impression
- more upright
- more symmetrical
- more composed
- more measured in gesture and stance

Not dramatically inhuman. The distinction is mostly through:
- body language
- face shape
- material language
- grooming and presentation
- silhouette

## Face and Presence
Facial structure should feel:
- refined
- intentional
- a little idealized
- not soft, but controlled

They should look like a people shaped by long-lived courtly, ritual, and scholarly traditions.

## Material Language
The Arrivals should feel sacred-classical and frontier-rustic at the same time.

Use:
- carved hardwood
- polished stone
- brass or bronze
- enamel
- lacquer
- structured woven cloth
- parchment and chart-like materials
- lenses, star tables, and carved instruments
- limited etched crystal or glass as ritual/scientific tools

Avoid:
- glowing hard-light surfaces
- overt sci-fi materials
- too much sleek minimalism

## Clothing Language
The Arrivals should look like:
- astrologer-nobles
- philosopher-officers
- ritual surveyors
- sacred navigators
- wardens on an expedition

Use:
- layered coats and tabards
- sashes and clasp systems
- structured hems
- geometric embroidery or trim
- mantle-like shoulder shapes
- rank through arrangement and pattern, not flashy technology

The clothing should feel formal even on the frontier.

## Geometry and Sacred Measure
The Arrivals’ affinity for geometry and celestial order should feel mystical, not modern-scientific.

Geometry is:
- liturgical
- lawful
- proportionate
- holy
- bound to truth and order

It should not feel like:
- equations for their own sake
- hard science fiction
- cold mechanical rationalism

## Architecture Language
Arrival frontier architecture should feel like a rustic branch of a sacred-classical tradition.

Use:
- raised platforms
- aligned layouts
- measured spacing
- formal pavilions
- carved posts and anchor stones
- observatory-like instruments
- modular but ceremonially proportioned structures
- structured cloth canopies
- timber and cloth used with deliberate symmetry

Their camps should say:
“we are far from home, but we still build according to law.”

## Contrast with Natives
The Natives are:
- rooted
- weathered
- materially organic
- asymmetrical
- memory-based

The Arrivals are:
- measured
- formal
- symmetrical
- crafted
- ceremonial
- orderly

This contrast should be readable immediately.

## Relationship to Other Peoples
### Toward Natives
Complicated:
- some see them as people to understand and work with
- some see them as backwards or overly governed by taboo
- some genuinely respect their relationship to place
- some see them as politically necessary

### Toward Severed
The Severed are not just enemies. They are a living internal scandal.
They are:
- kin
- heretics
- oathbreakers
- an old wound
- and possibly a warning

That should affect Arrival tone and politics.

## Visual Summary
The Arrivals are a sacred-classical, long-lived people whose visual language is built from:
- formal posture
- measured silhouette
- astronomical and geometric ornament
- polished and structured materials
- expeditionary but ceremonial architecture
- mystical order rather than sci-fi polish

## Avoid
- sci-fi sleekness
- obvious Roman/Greek copies
- pure “elegant elf” fantasy shorthand
- overtly glowing high-tech surfaces
- industrial modernity
