# World Overview v0

## Core Tone
The world is not a morality tale about nature, purity, civilization, colonization, or simple good vs evil.

It should feel like a living frontier world where cultures mix, trade, mistrust, imitate, ally, worship, resent, and adapt.

The world should feel:
- old
- beautiful
- uneasy
- politically messy
- spiritually deep
- full of small human truths

## Core Premise
This is a semi-alien, mystical world shaped by an ancient catastrophe known as the Age of Ash.

Long before the current story, a branch of the Arrivals came to this world shortly before the Age of Ash. At that time, the native peoples were already living through signs of catastrophe and performing rites, prayers, and warnings against what was coming.

That first Arrival branch was under ancient doctrine not to interfere, but it believed a god or revelation commanded otherwise. They intervened. They failed.

Their arrival was remembered as a mythic Fall. In native memory, some saw them as saviors or gods. Others later remembered them as liars, betrayers, false protectors, or oathbreakers.

The survivors became the Severed.

## The Arrivals
The Arrivals are a long-lived or semi-immortal people from another world.

They come from a sacred-classical civilization shaped by law, proportion, metaphysical order, and non-interference. They are aware of other life and other worlds, but do not normally intervene directly.

The modern Arrivals return because evidence suggests the Severed still survive and are active again. This allows them to justify intervention as an internal reckoning rather than normal foreign interference.

## The Natives
The Natives are a wetland-rooted people who later became heavily highland-adapted after the Age of Ash.

They are not anti-magic. They are anti-hubris. Their worldview is rooted in restraint, sacred place, warning, lived knowledge, memory, and relationship to land and ancient surviving lineages.

They preserve fractured memory of the Age of Ash through oral tradition, sacred geography, ritual, and taboo.

Different native communities remember the first Arrivals and the Severed differently. Some see the new Arrivals with curiosity or reverence; others with suspicion or hostility.

## The Severed
The Severed are the descendants or survivors of that original Arrival offshoot.

After failing to stop the catastrophe, they abandoned reliance on the old power and survived through:
- secrecy
- discipline
- engineering
- containment
- pressure-based technology
- adaptation to harsh environments

When the modern Arrivals return, dormant pathways of old power begin to reawaken. The Severed now have both their survival technology and access again to powers they once abandoned, making them the most dangerous organized force in the current setting.

## The Age of Ash
The Age of Ash was a world-shaping catastrophe, likely tied in part to massive volcanic or supervolcanic activity, but its full truth remains unresolved.

Its scars remain visible in:
- sulfuric valleys
- ash basins
- calderas
- lava tubes
- dangerous lowlands
- volcanic uplands
- altered ecosystems
- surviving ancient wildlife lineages

## The Deeper Force
There is one underlying deeper force or sacred reality in the setting, but different peoples relate to it differently.

The key rule is:
one force, different relationships

Broadly:
- Arrivals tend toward shaping, order, law, and formalization
- Natives tend toward reverence, attunement, restraint, and lived relationship

The exact truth remains unresolved.

## Starter Region World Structure
The first implementation is on the east coast of the northern landmass of the western continent.

That western continent is itself a paired landmass:
- a larger northern landmass
- a smaller southern landmass
- a narrow channel between them
- volcanic islands in the channel

The starter slice includes:
- alien redwood-like east coast
- inland wet fungal basin homeland
- foothills
- mountain spur
- first convergence city
- ash frontier beyond
- true main mountain range farther inland

## Project World Rules
- Alien but believable
- Biomes must flow logically
- No simple race morality
- No sci-fi drift for Arrivals
- No generic steampunk drift for Severed
- Wetland memory remains visible in native culture even in the mountains
- The world should feel older than the player
- The world should feel large before it becomes large in implementation
